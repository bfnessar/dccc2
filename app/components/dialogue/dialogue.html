Introduction

As part of my application for the role of Front-End Developer, I submit this single-page application. This application pulls data from a few JSON feeds (sources appear at the end of the presentation) and attempts to "tell a meaningful story".

After browsing the suggested data sources (all of which are provided by catalog.data.gov), I decided to focus my research on opioid-related deaths in the United States.

About the "Opioid Epidemic"

The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services refers to this phenomenon as the "Opioid Epidemic" on its page on the topic.

If you Google "opioid epidemic", you can see that the topic has been written about by such sources as The Economist, the Center for Disease Control and Prevention, BBC, the Washington Post, the New York Times, and CNN.

Here are five videos on the subject by CSPAN.

So there's a lot of commentary on this subject. There's probably lots of fascinating socioeconomic implications of this phenomenon. Smarter people than me have said plenty about it, and I implore you to research the topic. People die from opioid overdoses every day, and it is a tragedy.

That said, I'm not really here to provide any kind of in-depth analysis. I don't have any groundbreaking theories or meaningful political analysis on this subject. If you've already heard of the opioid epidemic, then I won't be telling you anything you don't already know. I'm not a historian, a sociologist, or a statistician. I'm simply here to demonstrate my ability to deploy a single-page website that can make REST-based API calls using AngularJS.

Presentation Format

At first I wasn't sure how I would format this presentation. I did some research, and found this article on Vox. The format of the article is:

  • Make an assertion.
  • Present a graph that supports that assertion
  • Provide commentary that elaborates on the graph and maybe even makes further assertions based on the graphed data.

Seems like a good model. I have one minor adjustment, however.

Like I said a moment ago, I'm not here to make assertions. Let's instead call it a hypothesis. I'll make a claim, we'll query some data, and we'll see if the data supports that claim.

Hypothesis: The Opioid Overdose Epidemic Especially Affects White Americans

I think I first heard this theory on C-SPAN. The idea was at least floating around in my head when I was thinking about forming a hypothesis. I did a bit of Googling and confirmed that this idea didn't come from nowhere.

This article by Modern Healthcare asserts that the rate of opioid-related deaths has increased at a higher rate for white Americans than for minority groups.

This slideshow, created by (someone at) the FDA, claims that low-income white males, roughly between the ages of 25-54, are an at-risk group.

Disclaimer

Now, I haven't built the part of the website that queries data yet. I'm writing this page on the fly. So before we get into any numbers, I'd like to remind you that this application is just an exercise in web development. I don't think I'm smarter than the FDA or CDC or anyone who researches this stuff for a living. If my data comes back and seemingly disproves the hypothesis, that's not me saying that these people are wrong. Likewise, if my data supports the hypothesis, that's not me saying they're right. There's lots of rigorous standards for statistical sampling, and that's important because data can be misleading.

I'm just here to demonstrate my ability to make REST-based API calls.
